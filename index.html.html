01 / nCount
nCount
Combining emerging technology and systems approach for people-centric sustainability action at scale
What it is

nCount represents a cutting-edge approach to large scale prosperity and social well-being, through environmental monitoring and conservation. Specifically designed to engage forest-dwelling communities, citizen scientists, students, nature enthusiasts, and researchers; participants actively contribute to the scientific understanding of biodiversity by documenting plant and animal species in their local ecosystems.

nCount harnesses the power of AI and computer vision to assist in the identification and classification of species from images, ensuring accurate and efficient data processing.

Key Features
  • Multi-stakeholder design, built for communities, civil society, public agencies, and researchers
  • Supports biodiversity monitoring, carbon sequestration, NRM, and value chain use cases
  • AI/ML-driven analytics converting field data into ground-implementable insights
  • Available on web and mobile (Android)

06 / Glade
Glade
LLM-powered pipeline for image de-duplication, validation, and dataset cleaning
Phase 1 · Duplicate Elimination
  • Exact Duplicates via SHA-256 cryptographic hash — byte-for-byte identical images are detected and removed instantly.
  • HSV Histogram + Cosine Similarity — generates a colour fingerprint of each image and measures colour-space similarity between pairs to group visually similar images.
  • pHash + Embeddings — perceptual hash captures the structural pattern of an image; combined with embedding-based similarity to group near-duplicate images that differ in lighting or minor composition.
Phase 2 · Label Validation & Quality Check
  • Single LLM — fast first-pass validation of image labels against content
  • Multi-LLM Consensus — OpenAI + Gemini + Claude cross-validate; agreement required to confirm label quality

08 / Haat Infrastructure Planning
Mapping Rural Markets
for Better Infrastructure
Finding the Gaps: Where Rural Markets Are Needed Most
What it is

The Samastipur Haat Study is a spatial analysis project identifying underserved rural areas in Samastipur district for potential new haat (rural market) establishment. Using satellite-derived data and network analysis, it builds an evidence base for infrastructure planning, incorporating resilience factors to ensure long-term viability of proposed sites.

Methodology: Three Key Factors
  • Nightlight Intensity: proxy for economic activity and electrification to identify low-development zones
  • Population Density: number of people living per 1 hectare area
  • Network-based Distance: travel distance/time to existing haats via road network
Site Selection Approach
  • Cluster identification in underserved areas based on the three-factor framework
  • Statistical approach for site selection and prioritization across candidate locations
  • Flood risk assessment layered in to evaluate suitability and long-term resilience of proposed haat sites
Output
  • Spatially prioritized list of candidate haat locations
  • Flood-resilient site recommendations
  • Evidence base ready for policy and infrastructure planning use
